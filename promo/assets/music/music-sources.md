# Background Music Sources

Royalty-free music for promotional videos. Keep music subtle — it supports the voiceover, never competes with it.

## Volume Level
- **Background music:** -18 to -20 dB under the voiceover track
- **During voiceover:** Keep music low and steady
- **During transitions/intros:** Music can be slightly louder (-12 to -15 dB) when there's no voice
- In OBS: Add music as a Media Source, adjust volume in the Audio Mixer

## Recommended Genres
- Lo-fi / chill beats — relaxed, non-distracting
- Ambient electronic — modern, tech-forward feel
- Minimal tech / corporate — clean, professional
- Avoid: lyrics, heavy bass, dramatic builds (this isn't a gaming channel)

## Free Sources (No Attribution Required)

### YouTube Audio Library
- **URL:** https://studio.youtube.com/channel/UC/music (accessible from YouTube Studio)
- **License:** Free for YouTube videos, no attribution needed
- **Best for:** YouTube tutorials
- **How to use:** Download tracks directly, filter by genre/mood/duration

### Pixabay Music
- **URL:** https://pixabay.com/music/
- **License:** Free for all platforms, no attribution required
- **Best for:** TikTok and Twitter clips (YouTube Audio Library tracks are technically YouTube-only)
- **How to use:** Search by mood (e.g., "chill", "corporate", "ambient"), download MP3

### Uppbeat
- **URL:** https://uppbeat.io
- **License:** Free tier — 10 downloads/month, attribution required in description
- **Best for:** Higher quality tracks when free sources don't have what you need

## Paid Source (Best Quality)

### Epidemic Sound
- **URL:** https://www.epidemicsound.com
- **Price:** ~$15/month (Personal plan)
- **License:** All platforms, no attribution, no copyright claims
- **Best for:** Professional-quality music with no risk of content ID flags
- **Worth it if:** You're posting 2+ videos per week and want zero copyright hassle

## How to Add Music in OBS
1. In your scene, click `+` in Sources > `Media Source`
2. Name it "Background Music"
3. Browse to your downloaded track
4. Check "Loop" if the track is shorter than your video
5. In the Audio Mixer, drag the volume slider for the music source to -18 to -20 dB
6. Record a test — make sure your voice is clearly louder than the music
